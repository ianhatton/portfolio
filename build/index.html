CSS

The art of presenting beautiful content for the web is something that still excites me to this day.

CSS is the area of my job that always makes the biggest initial impact and it's for this reason that I take great care in making sure that my responsive builds look fantastic and feel intuitive—no matter what platform they're being viewed on.

In my opinion, what's going on "under the hood" is just as important as what's presented in the browser, which is why I'm quite meticulous when it comes to organising my work and being as modular as possible. Spaghetti is good, but CSS spaghetti tastes bad.

I have made great relationships with the designers I have worked with in the past and I'm always happy to provide guidance and constructive criticism where it's due in order to deliver a stunning* final product.

*exact level of stunningness dependent on design.

JavaScript

JavaScript is, without doubt, the most challenging and, at times, frustrating part of my job. Having said that, I also find it the most rewarding.

For me, it's an area of constant learning and improvement, and I've found that the way I write my code has changed greatly over the years. There's nothing like that moment where the penny drops and something turns from a fuzzy understanding into solid knowledge.

I have contributed to a number of projects, each utilising different setups, so I have been exposed to several of the front-running libraries. With the current state of JavaScript development and the "flavour of the month" scenario that seems so prevalent, I'm of the opinion that there's no better time to hone my vanilla JavaScript skills, and so it's in this area that I am focusing my efforts.

I have written a number of vanilla JavaScript modules which are in use across several of my recent projects, and I'm looking forward to expanding my knowledge with each new project I work on.
